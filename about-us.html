About MNCFC

Mahalanobis National Crop Forecast Centre (MNCFC) has been established under the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers’ Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers’ Welfare, Government of India for applications of space and geospatial technology in various domains of agriculture. The Centre, named after the great Indian Statistician, Dr. Prashant Chandra Mahalanobis, was inaugurated on 23rd April, 2012.

The Mahalanobis National Crop Forecast Centre (MNCFC) has evolved significantly since its inception. Initially, it focused on providing timely crop forecasts and drought assessments using cutting-edge techniques developed by the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). Over the past decade, MNCFC's role has expanded considerably. It now serves as a key technical advisor to the Ministry, promoting the use of satellite remote sensing and GIS technologies to improve crop estimation. Essentially, MNCFC has transitioned from a specialized agricultural forecasting unit to a broader government resource for leveraging space-based technologies in agriculture.

The MNCFC's current work encompasses a range of crucial agricultural monitoring and assessment activities. Key projects include: crop production forecasting (pre-harvest) at national, state, and district levels for major crops under the FASAL project; drought assessment and monitoring, including support to states; analyzing the impact of events like hailstorms, heavy rainfall, and floods on crop areas; monitoring crop condition via remote sensing; studying harvest patterns; tracking crop residue burning; identifying changes in cropping patterns compared to the previous year; providing weekly crop condition updates; and supporting crop insurance (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) through satellite data analysis for loss assessment, area discrepancy resolution, yield dispute resolution, risk zoning, and seasonality mapping.

Krishi decision Support System (K-DSS) is a recent initiative which act as a digital platform to speed up geospatial/digital innovations/solutions in agriculture sector.

Towards implementation of these activities, the Centre collaborates with various state departments, ISRO, other research institutes, academia and industry .
