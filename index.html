Mahalanobis National Crop Forecast Centre

Welcome to Mahalanobis National Crop Forecast Centre of Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers welfare, Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India. The Centre, named after great Indian Statistician P. C. Mahalanobis has been established to operationalize the use of space and related technology for better agricultural forecasting and drought assessment. It was inaugurated on 23rd April, 2012 by Hon'ble Agriculture Minister.

Major Projects

Forecasting Agricultural output using Space, Agro-meteorology and Land based observations (FASAL)

Under the operational component of FASAL programme of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, MNCFC regularly generates Remote Sensing based crop production forecasts at District/State/National level for major crops of the country and submit to Ministry.

Read More...

Drought Monitoring

MNCFC regularly monitors the agricultural area of the country using various satellite data indicators such as Normalized difference vegetation index(NDVI), Soil Moisture etc. and provide inputs to the Crop weather watch group(CWWG) of the Ministry.

Read More...

Krishi Decision Support System

Unified Digital platform to speed up Geo-Spatial Solutions in Indian Agriculture. An indigenous, state of the art, geospatial platform with open source technologies, hosting multiple datasets, performing deep analytics of such data, producing and disseminating different information products and services to different stakeholders.

Read More...

Support to PMFBY

MNCFC is provdiing all the technological support required for implementaion of Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) of Ministry. Various technolgical initiatives have been taken place such as YES-TECH, WINDS and CROPIC.

About MNCFC

The Centre has been established to provide in-season crop forecasts and assessment of drought situation using state of the art techniques and methodologies developed by Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
