About FASAL

The Mahalanobis National Crop Forecast Centre (MNCFC), under the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare's FASAL program, plays a crucial role in Crop Production Forecasting for India. Using methodologies developed by the Space Applications Centre (SAC), ISRO, the MNCFC generates crop forecasts at District, State, and National levels for major crops of the country. These forecasts rely on both optical and microwave remote sensing data to estimate crop acreage, assess crop condition, and predict production.

The program covers a wide range of crops, including wheat, rice, mustard, sugarcane, jute, rabi sorghum, cotton, soybean, tur, gram, and lentil, for which crop maps and acreage statistics are produced.Recently, the center has also started generating a Crop Health Factor for wheat and paddy.

MNCFC collaborates with ISRO and IARI (ICAR) for operational yield estimates using semi-physical and crop simulation models. Since Kharif 2024-25, private agencies have also been involved for providing Crop acreage and maps for Cotton, Tur, Soybean, Gram, Mustard and Lentil along with Ground Truth data

Since Kharif 2024-25, KrishiMapper App developed by the department is being used for collecting polygon based Ground Truth data. Around 90000+ in Kharif 2024-25 and 100000+ in Rabi 2024-25, Ground Truth surveys have been conducted which have been used for Crop Mapping
